# Jyuroku Rakan (Juroku Rakan)

## Overview
This is a historic site in Yuza Town where 22 Buddhist figures are carved into the coastal rock formations. A local Buddhist monk carved them over five years during the Meiji era, praying for maritime safety and the salvation of all people. Their appearance against the backdrop of the rough waves of the Sea of Japan is truly solemn.

## Highlights & Features
- **Rock-Carved Buddhas**: Figures such as Shakyamuni, Monju and Fugen Bodhisattvas, and Arhats (Rakan) are masterfully carved into the natural rock face.
- **Evening Scenery**: The silhouette of the Jyuroku Rakan rocks against the setting sun over the Sea of Japan is known as a superb photo spot.
- **Historical Background**: This is a historical monument embodying the faith and prayers for safety of the local fishermen.

## Basic Information
- **Location**: Fukura, Yuza, Akumi District, Yamagata Prefecture
- **Hours**: 24 hours
- **Fee**: Free

## Access
- **By Car**: About 15 minutes from the Yuza-Chokai IC on the Nihonkai-Tohoku Expressway via National Route 7.
- **By Public Transport**: About a 10-minute walk from JR Fukura Station.
